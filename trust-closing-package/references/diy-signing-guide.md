## Part 1: Step-by-Step Instructions

### STEP BY STEP INSTRUCTIONS

#### Review All Documents Before Signing
- Carefully read through each document to ensure:
  - Your name is correct.
  - The names of all fiduciaries (e.g., trustee, executor, agents) are spelled correctly.
  - You understand each document's purpose.
- Do not sign or initial anything until you are with the Notary and, if needed, your witnesses.

#### Update the date of each document
- Date all documents to reflect the date that the documents will be signed.
- It is crucial to date each document correctly with the day, month, and year of the signing.

#### Schedule Your Signing Appointment
- Arrange for a time and location where you can be present with:
  - A Notary Public (required for certain documents).
  - Two (2) independent adult witnesses (required for certain documents like your Will, Durable Power of Attorney, Health Care Proxy and Advance Directive).
- All parties must be present in the same room at the same time when you sign.

#### Witness Requirements
- Each witness must:
  - Be at least 18 years old.
  - Not be named in your estate planning documents.
  - Not be a beneficiary or related to anyone named in your plan.
- Avoid using the spouse or partner of any named beneficiary.
- Your notary must be a separate person from your two witnesses; the same individual cannot both witness and notarize your documents.

#### Gather What You Need
- Before the signing, have the following ready:
  - Your full set of estate planning documents.
  - A blue ink pen (preferred for signing).
  - A valid, government-issued photo ID to show the Notary.

#### Executing the Documents
- In the presence of the Notary (and witnesses if required):
  - Update the date on each document to reflect the actual day of signing (Month, Day, Year).
  - Sign each document in blue ink, where indicated.
  - Allow the Notary to notarize and stamp where required.
  - For documents needing witnesses:
    - They must observe you signing.
    - Then, they must sign in the designated witness sections.

#### After Signing
- Double-check that:
  - All documents are signed and dated correctly.
  - All required signatures (yours, notary, witnesses) are complete.
- **Scan and email** a copy of the signed estate plan to us for our records.
  - You may use the Adobe Scan app or another mobile scanning tool if you don't have access to a scanner.

#### Storing the Originals
- Store your original, signed documents in a safe but accessible location, such as:
  - A fireproof home safe.
  - In a secure file known to your fiduciaries.

## Part 2: Checklist

### CHECKLIST

#### Step 1: Review Your Documents Carefully
- Read through each document fully.
- Make sure your name and the names of your fiduciaries (e.g., personal representative, trustee, agents) are spelled correctly.
- Note which documents require notarization and which also require witnesses.
- Do not make handwritten changes to the documents unless instructed.

#### Step 2: Arrange for a Signing Appointment
- Schedule a time when you, a Notary Public, and two independent adult witnesses can all be present at the same time. Your witnesses must be over 18, not related to you, not beneficiaries, and not named anywhere in your estate documents. Avoid using spouses of beneficiaries or anyone who could benefit from your estate.
- Banks and credit unions may offer complimentary notary services. Your local Town Hall, UPS Store, and AAA may have notary services as well. Alternatively, you can hire a mobile notary to come to your location by searching "mobile notary in Massachusetts".
- Ensure the location is quiet and comfortable so you can take your time signing correctly.

#### Step 3: Gather the Required Items
- All original estate planning documents.
- A valid, government-issued photo ID to show the notary.
- A blue ink pen (blue ink is preferred to clearly show originals).
- A printed copy of this checklist and the Estate Planning Signing Instructions, if provided.

#### Step 4: Sign Your Documents
- Everyone (you, the notary, and both witnesses) must be in the room **at the same time** when you begin signing.
- Update the date on each document to reflect the actual day of signing. Month Day, Year (ex. January 1, 2025) / Day day of Month Year (ex. 1st day of January 2025)
- Sign each document in blue ink, in the designated spaces.
- Ensure the Notary Public notarizes where required (they may also need to sign and stamp additional pages).
- For documents requiring witnesses: (1) The witnesses must observe you sign the document and (2) then each witness must sign in the appropriate witness section.

#### Step 5: Double Check Everything
- Confirm that all required signatures are present: yours, the notary's, and the witnesses' (if applicable).
- Double-check that the correct date appears on every document.
- Ensure all pages are in the correct order and nothing is missing.

#### Step 6: Send a Copy for Records
- Scan and email a complete, signed copy of your estate plan for our records.
  - Tip: You can use the Adobe Scan app (free for iPhone and Android) to scan documents with your phone.
- Keep the original signed documents in a safe but accessible place, such as a fireproof home safe or with your attorney.

## Witness Signature Block Reference

The witness section in the documents is formatted as follows (for attorney reference — clients fill this in during signing):

> We, [Witness One Name] and [Witness Two Name], the witnesses, each declare in the presence of [CLIENT NAME], that [CLIENT NAME] signed this Declaration (or directed another to sign the Declaration) in the presence of each of us, that the Principal signed it willingly (or directed another to sign it), that each of us signs this Declaration as witness in the presence of the Principal, and that to the best of our knowledge the Principal is eighteen (18) years of age or over, of sound mind, and under no constraint or undue influence.

Witnesses fill in: name and address in the signature lines provided.

## Notary Block Reference

The notary section reads:

> COMMONWEALTH OF MASSACHUSETTS  
> COUNTY OF [COUNTY WHERE DOCUMENTS ARE SIGNED]  
> On this [DATE], before me, the undersigned notary public, personally appeared [CLIENT NAME], proved to me through satisfactory evidence of identification, which was a government-issued photo identification, to be the persons whose names are signed on the preceding document and acknowledged to me that they signed it voluntarily for its stated purpose.  
> WITNESS my hand and notarial seal.  
> My Commission Expires: [NOTARY EXPIRATION DATE]
