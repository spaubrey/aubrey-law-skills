# Joint Revocable Trust — Drafting Guide

Load this reference when drafting a Revocable Trust for a married couple.

Source template: `Assets/joint-trust.docx`

---

## Document Identity

- **File name:** `YYYY-MM-DD_[Trust-Name]_[CLIENT-LAST-NAME].docx`
- **Page size:** US Letter (12240 × 15840 DXA)
- **Margins:** Top 1080, Bottom 1080, Left 1008, Right 1008 DXA
- **Font:** Garamond throughout, 12pt body, 14pt bold titles
- **Color:** Black `000000` only — no navy, no teal, no gray
- **Header:** None — no logo, no header image
- **Footer:** Plain black text, document title and page number

## Opening Recital

Joint trust — always two grantors and at least two trustees:

- No initial co-trustee:
  `The parties to this trust are [CLIENT FULL NAME] and [SPOUSE FULL NAME]
  (together, the Grantors) and [CLIENT FULL NAME] and [SPOUSE FULL NAME]
  (together, the Trustees).`

- With initial co-trustee:
  `The parties to this trust are [CLIENT FULL NAME] and [SPOUSE FULL NAME]
  (together, the Grantors) and [CLIENT FULL NAME], [SPOUSE FULL NAME], and
  [CO-TRUSTEE FULL NAME] (collectively, the Trustees).`

---

## Identifying the Trust (Section 1.01)

- No co-trustee:
  `"[CLIENT FULL NAME] and [SPOUSE FULL NAME], as Trustees of the [TRUST NAME]
  dated _______________, 20___, and any amendments thereto."`

- With co-trustee:
  `"[CLIENT FULL NAME], [SPOUSE FULL NAME], and [CO-TRUSTEE FULL NAME],
  as Trustees of the [TRUST NAME] dated _______________, 20___, and any
  amendments thereto."`

---

## Key Placeholder Substitutions

| Placeholder | Value |
|---|---|
| `[TRUST NAME]` | Full trust name (e.g., "The John and Jane Smith Revocable Trust") |
| `[CLIENT FULL NAME]` | First spouse full name |
| `[SPOUSE FULL NAME]` | Second spouse full name |
| `[CO-TRUSTEE FULL NAME]` | Initial co-trustee name (if any) |
| `[GRANTOR 1]` / `[GRANTOR 2]` | Same as CLIENT / SPOUSE |
| Date line | Blank line or filled date |

---

## Pronoun and Number Rules

Joint trust = PLURAL throughout when referring to both grantors:
- "we", "us", "our" in grantor voice
- "the Grantors", "the Trustees" (plural)
- Individual provisions (e.g., one spouse's death) use singular for that
  specific spouse

**Critical:** Many provisions use "a Grantor" (singular = either one).
Do not change those to plural — they are intentional.

---

## Trustee Succession (Article Three)

For joint trusts, succession typically works as follows:
1. Surviving spouse continues as sole Trustee
2. Named successor trustee(s) upon death or incapacity of surviving spouse

Fill in:
- `[SUCCESSOR TRUSTEE 1]` — primary successor name
- `[SUCCESSOR TRUSTEE 2]` — alternate successor (if provided)

---

## Family Information (Article Two)

- Both spouses named
- Children listed (names, DOBs if minor)
- Cross-reference to both grantors' family provisions

---

## Revocation and Amendment

Joint trust requires both grantors to act during joint lifetime.
After first death, trust becomes irrevocable as to deceased grantor's share.
Template handles this automatically — do not alter these provisions.

## Notary Blocks

One notary block per signatory (2 minimum; 3 if co-trustee).
See notary block format in `individual-trust-guide.md`.
Use correct pronoun (he/she) for each individual.
