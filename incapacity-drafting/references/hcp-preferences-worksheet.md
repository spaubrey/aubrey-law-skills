# Source: Scott Aubrey / Aubrey Law actual template (HCP_Preferences_Worksheet.docx)

## PURPOSE & CHARACTER OF THIS DOCUMENT

This is a **client-facing discussion guide**, not a legally binding instrument.
It is intended to help the client articulate health care preferences in advance
of conversations with their Health Care Agent. It is delivered alongside the
HCP as a companion document.

From the template's own disclaimer:

> This worksheet is for your personal use and reference. It is not part of
> your Health Care Proxy and is not legally binding. The notes you make below
> are intended to guide your Health Care Agent and are not directives that
> your Agent must follow.

Because this is not legally binding:
- No witnesses, no notary
- Single client signature at bottom
- All eleven topic tables emit with an empty `*My notes:*` row for the client
  to handwrite or type into

## PRODUCTION RULES (apply when emitting the document)

- **Names in signature footer:** Replace `[CLIENT FULL NAME]` with the client's
  full legal name in **UPPERCASE plain text**.
- **Date:** `[DATE]` left blank for the client to fill in at the time of their
  conversation. Do NOT pre-fill with today's date — the client may complete
  the worksheet over time.
- **Topic tables:** Emit all eleven tables in the order shown below with the
  framing question(s) verbatim. The `*My notes:*` cell is intentionally empty
  and italicized.
- **Formatting:** Each topic uses a two-row table:
  - Row 1 (header): topic name, bold
  - Row 2 (body): framing question(s) explaining what to discuss
  - Row 3 (notes): `*My notes:*` label only; remainder of cell is blank space
  
  In the source template the "header" and "framing" rows are combined into a
  single two-row layout (header row + framing+notes row). Preserve the source
  layout when emitting.
- **No al_generator article helpers needed.** This document is structurally
  flat — no articles, no sections. Render via body() and table helpers.

Then:

Important Information to Discuss with Your Health Care Agent

Your Health Care Agent has broad authority to make health care decisions for

is important that your Health Care Agent know your wishes regarding the
topics below. Use this worksheet as a starting point for that conversation.
Discuss your preferences periodically so your Agent always knows your current
views.

This worksheet is for your personal use and reference. It is not part of

## TOPIC TABLES (eleven, in order)

For each topic emit a table with:
- Header row: topic name (bold)
- Framing row: the question(s) verbatim
- Notes row: `*My notes:*` (italics) followed by empty space for client input

### 1. Life Support
> Life support is a treatment or procedure given when a patient's body cannot
> keep itself alive (for example, mechanical ventilation, dialysis, or
> cardiac support). Under what circumstances would you want life support?
> For what duration? Are there circumstances in which you would not want
> life support?

### 2. Intubation and Mechanical Ventilation
> If a patient cannot maintain an open airway, a breathing tube can be
> inserted to allow a ventilator to assist with breathing. Intubation can be
> a temporary measure to treat a curable condition or a longer-term
> intervention. Under what circumstances would you not want to be intubated,
> if any?

### 3. Artificial Nutrition and Hydration
> When a patient is no longer able to eat and drink on their own, nutrition
> and hydration can be administered artificially (for example, by feeding
> tube or IV). Under what circumstances would you want, or not want,
> artificial nutrition and hydration? For how long?

### 4. Surgery
> Surgeries can range from minor to major depending on the underlying
> condition. Are there circumstances in which you would not want surgery
> (for example, due to age, comorbidities, or quality-of-life
> considerations)?

### 5. Amputation
> Would you consent to having a limb partially or fully amputated? Would
> your wishes differ depending on which limb is involved or the medical
> circumstances?

### 6. Medication
> Do you have objections to any particular type of medication, method of
> administration, or duration of use? How would you weigh medications with
> significant adverse side effects against their benefits?

### 7. Blood Transfusion
> Through a blood transfusion, donated blood is administered to a patient.
> Do you have any objections to receiving a blood transfusion?

### 8. Care Facilities and In-Home Care
> Care options include assisted living, nursing homes, continuum-of-care
> communities, and in-home care. Do you have a preference among these
> options? Do preferences depend on location, services offered, type of
> room, or cost? How important is it to you to remain in your home?

### 9. Religious, Moral, or Spiritual Preferences
> Are there health care preferences or restrictions that arise from your
> religious, moral, or spiritual beliefs?

### 10. Pain Management and Comfort Care
> What are your priorities regarding pain management at the end of life?
> Are you willing to accept medications that may shorten life if they
> meaningfully relieve suffering?

### 11. Other
> Health care encompasses a wide range of procedures and treatments. Are
> there other preferences, values, or circumstances you want your Health
> Care Agent to know about?
