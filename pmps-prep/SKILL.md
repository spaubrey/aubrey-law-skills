# Peace of Mind Planning Session (PMPS) — Document Preparation Skill

You are preparing the pre-meeting document package for an Aubrey Law estate planning consultation. Scott uses these documents to run structured, professional client meetings where he presents his analysis, recommendations, and pricing.

## What This Skill Produces

Four .docx documents, all generated by running the existing JS templates in the `Templates/` folder against a populated data object:

| Document | Template File | Audience | Purpose |
|----------|--------------|----------|---------|
| Consultation Agenda | `pmps-agenda.js` | Attorney-only | Scott's meeting roadmap with talking points, flags, and discussion notes |
| Estate Plan Proposal | `pmps-proposal.js` | Client-facing | Multi-page proposal covering analysis, recommendations, fiduciary appointments, pricing |
| MA Estate Tax Worksheet | `pmps-tax-worksheet.js` | Attorney-only | Step-by-step tax calculation showing both scenarios with full rate table |
| One-Page Plan Summary | `pmps-one-page-summary.js` | Client-facing | Single-page overview with tax comparison, document list, and pricing table |

All four are generated by default. Scott can say "skip the agenda" or "just the client docs" to exclude specific documents.

## Legal Compliance Guardrails

These guardrails apply to every output this skill produces. They exist because AI-generated legal documents carry real risk if treated as final work product without attorney review.

- **Cite specific document sections, never summarize from memory.** When referencing a client's intake data, questionnaire answers, or financial figures, point to exactly where the information came from (e.g., "per the intake questionnaire" or "from the uploaded spreadsheet, cell B12"). If you're unsure where a data point originated, say so.
- **Flag uncertainty rather than guessing.** If client data is ambiguous, incomplete, or seems inconsistent (e.g., asset totals don't add up, fiduciary names are unclear, ages don't match DOBs), flag it explicitly for Scott's review. Use a `[VERIFY: ...]` tag in the generation report. Never silently fill in plausible-sounding values.
- **Never present AI output as authoritative legal research.** The tax computations, document recommendations, and planning analysis in these documents are drafts for Scott's review. They reflect the MA estate tax structure as coded in the templates but do not constitute legal advice.
- **Preserve original document language when quoting.** If the intake questionnaire or uploaded document uses specific phrasing (e.g., a client's stated wishes, a beneficiary designation), carry that language through verbatim rather than paraphrasing.
- **Include disclaimer that output requires attorney review.** Every generation report should note that all documents are drafts pending Scott's review and approval before client delivery.

## Step-by-Step Workflow

### Step 0: Clarify Legal Insurance and Fees

Before building the data object, confirm two things if they're not clear from the provided intake data:

1. **Does the client have a legal insurance plan?** (MetLife, ARAG, LegalShield, etc.) This drives the pricing logic in the proposal and one-page summary. If the intake data doesn't mention it, ask Scott: *"Does this client have a legal insurance plan (MetLife, ARAG, etc.)? This affects the pricing section."*

2. **If no legal plan — what is the flat fee?** When `hasLegalPlan` is false, the one-page summary and proposal show flat-fee pricing instead of plan coverage. Ask Scott: *"No legal plan detected. What flat fee should I use for the estate plan? (e.g., $3,500 for trust-based, $2,000 for will-only)"* Do not guess the fee — it varies by plan complexity.

Only proceed to document generation once both are confirmed or clearly stated in the input data.

### Step 1: Locate the Templates

The four template files and the data schema live in `Skills/pmps-prep-skill/Templates/` in the workspace:

```
Skills/pmps-prep-skill/
├── Templates/
│   ├── _shared.js               ← Shared branding, helpers, and docx utilities
│   ├── pmps-data-schema.js      ← Schema definition with SAMPLE and EMPTY objects
│   ├── pmps-agenda.js           ← Consultation Agenda template
│   ├── pmps-proposal.js         ← Estate Plan Proposal template
│   ├── pmps-tax-worksheet.js    ← MA Estate Tax Worksheet template
│   ├── pmps-one-page-summary.js ← One-Page Plan Summary template
│   ├── assets/
│   │   └── aubrey-law-logo.png  ← Logo for headers
│   └── node_modules/docx/       ← docx library (pre-installed)
└── references/
    └── ma-estate-tax-computation.md ← MA rate table and algorithm
```

Workspace OUTPUTS go to: `OUTPUTS/[ClientName]/`

Read `pmps-data-schema.js` first — it defines every field the templates expect, with a complete SAMPLE object and a blank EMPTY object.

**Plan structures (June 2026).** Set `plan.planStructure` to one of `joint_outright`, `joint_disclaimer`, `separate_clayton`, or `individual_single`. The schema exports a `SAMPLES` map (one per structure) plus the default `SAMPLE` (separate_clayton). The four templates branch on `planStructure` via `shared.resolvePlanStructure(data)`; if it is omitted they fall back to the legacy `hasCreditShelter` boolean. `plan.clayton` is true only for `separate_clayton`; `plan.maQtip` is true for `joint_disclaimer` and `separate_clayton`. The "both exemptions preserved" scenario shelters gross − $4,000,000 at the second death. All `tax` figures are NET of the $99,600 unified credit and are estimates pending M-706 verification. See `references/pmps-planstructure-mapping.md` and `references/ma-estate-tax-computation.md`.

### Step 2: Parse the Client Data

Scott will provide client information in one of these formats:

**Word document or PDF** — An intake questionnaire with client details, family info, assets, and preferences. Read the file and extract all relevant data.

**Excel spreadsheet** — Typically a financial summary with asset categories and values. May also include family info in separate tabs.

**Pasted or typed text** — Freeform notes, a filled-in questionnaire, or structured data typed directly into the conversation.

Your job is to map whatever Scott provides into the data schema. Here's what to extract:

**Required fields (the documents won't make sense without these):**
- Client name(s), type (couple vs. individual), address, DOBs
- Children (names, DOBs, ages, minor status)
- Asset inventory with values (enough to compute gross estate)
- Fiduciary appointments (trustees, personal reps, POA agents, HCP agents, guardians)
- Which documents are recommended

**Important flags to determine from context:**
- `hasMinorChildren` — any child under 18
- `hasLegalPlan` — does the client have a MetLife, ARAG, or similar legal plan?
- `hasTrust` — is this a trust-based plan?
- `hasCreditShelter` — gross estate over $2M for a couple? Recommend credit shelter.
- `hasRealEstate` — does the client own property to transfer to the trust?
- `hasLifeInsurance` — any life insurance policies?
- `hasAnticipatedInheritance` — expecting a future inheritance?
- `hasPets` — any pets to plan for?

**If data is missing:** Ask Scott. Don't fill gaps with assumptions — these are legal documents. The one exception: you can compute ages from DOBs, and you can determine `hasMinorChildren` from the children's ages.

### Step 3: Compute the MA Estate Tax

**Use the calculator — do not compute brackets by hand.** Hand-computation is how
gross figures end up where net belongs. Call:

```js
const { computeMATax } = require(path.join(TMPL, "pmps-tax-calc.js"));
data.tax = computeMATax(grossEstate, data.plan.planStructure);
```

`computeMATax(grossEstate, planStructure)` returns the fully-populated `tax`
object — net of the $99,600 unified credit, with the correct $4M shelter for the
two credit-shelter structures and `scenario2: null` for `joint_outright` /
`individual_single`. All intermediate fields the Tax Worksheet displays
(adjustedTaxableEstate, bracket info, excess, grossTax, netTax) are included.

Read `references/ma-estate-tax-computation.md` for the underlying methodology and
the rate table. All figures are estimates pending M-706 verification.

The tax computation works differently for couples vs. individuals, but **both get a Tax Worksheet document.** The worksheet is valuable for any estate over $2M because it shows the step-by-step calculation Scott walks clients through.

**By structure** (`computeMATax` handles each automatically):
- **`joint_outright`** — one scenario (full estate at second death, one exemption). scenario2 null, savings 0. Quantify the lost exemption.
- **`joint_disclaimer` / `separate_clayton`** — scenario1 (Without Planning) + scenario2 (both exemptions preserved; second-death taxable = gross − $4,000,000). Savings = scenario1.netTax − scenario2.totalNetTax.
- **`individual_single`** — one scenario (tax at death). scenario2 null, savings 0.

For estates under $2M (couple or individual), `computeMATax` returns all-$0 tax.

### Step 4: Build the Data Object

Construct the full data object matching the schema in `pmps-data-schema.js`. Pay attention to:

- **Display values**: Many fields have both a raw number and a formatted display string (e.g., `grossEstate: 12238552` and `grossEstateDisplay: "$12,238,552"`). Populate both.
- **Approximate values**: Fields like `grossEstateApprox` ("$12.2 million") and `netTaxApprox` ("$1,325,000") are for narrative text. Round sensibly.
- **Document list**: Build from the recommended plan. Each document has `name`, `perSpouse` (generates two copies for couples), and optionally `joint`. Count them for `documentCount`.
- **Considerations**: Populate the `considerations` object with relevant talking points — ILIT, gifting, beneficiary designations, inheritance planning, real estate transfer, young adult plans (especially for college-aged children — include HCP, DPOA, Education POA, Living Will, and HIPAA), pets.

### Step 5: Generate the Documents

The `docx` package is pre-installed in `Templates/node_modules/`. Run each template via bash:

```javascript
const TMPL = "/path/to/Skills/pmps-prep-skill/Templates";  // e.g. /Users/scottaubrey/Desktop/Vault/obsidian-aubrey law/Skills/pmps-prep-skill/Templates
const OUT  = "/path/to/OUTPUTS/ClientName/";          // create this dir first
const path = require("path");

const data = {
  matter: {
    filePrefix: "ClientLastName",   // used as filename prefix
    outputDir: OUT,                 // templates write files here
    date: "May 1, 2025",
    attorney: "Scott Aubrey, Esq."
  },
  // ... rest of data object
};

// Run all four templates — each writes a .docx to data.matter.outputDir
require(path.join(TMPL, "pmps-agenda.js"))(data);
require(path.join(TMPL, "pmps-proposal.js"))(data);
require(path.join(TMPL, "pmps-tax-worksheet.js"))(data);
require(path.join(TMPL, "pmps-one-page-summary.js"))(data);
```

**Important:** Set `data.matter.outputDir` to the full absolute path of the client's output folder and create it before running (`mkdir -p`). Set `data.matter.filePrefix` to the client's last name(s) (e.g., `"Duchin"`, `"Miller-Bethlehem"`).

The templates handle all formatting, branding, conditional sections, and file writing.

## Document Exclusion

**Default: always generate all four documents** — for both couples and individuals. The Agenda and Tax Worksheet are just as important for individual clients as for couples; the content adapts automatically.

Only exclude documents if Scott explicitly asks. Common patterns:
- "just the client docs" → proposal + one-page summary only
- "skip the agenda" → proposal + tax worksheet + summary
- "just the tax worksheet" → tax worksheet only

## Edge Cases and Judgment Calls

**Couple vs. Individual:** The templates handle both, driven by `clients.type`. All four documents are generated for both couples and individuals. For individuals, spouse-specific sections are omitted, per-spouse documents generate one copy instead of two, and the Tax Worksheet shows a single scenario (tax at death) rather than the two-scenario credit shelter comparison. The Agenda still serves as Scott's meeting roadmap — it just won't include credit shelter discussion points for individuals.

**Individual client fiduciary table — always render it.** Even when the client has no spouse, the Estate Plan Proposal must include a fiduciary appointments table. The naming of trustees, POA agents, and health care agents is just as critical for a single person as for a couple — and this table is the primary tool Scott uses to drive that conversation in the meeting. Use a 2-column layout (Role | Client's Appointments) populated from `data.fiduciaries.spouse1`. Never skip the fiduciary section for individual clients.

**Individual client data shape.** For individual clients:
- Set `clients.spouse2` to `null` (omit it — do not provide a stub)
- Set `fiduciaries.spouse2` to `null`
- All fiduciary appointments live under `fiduciaries.spouse1`, which represents the individual client
- The `clients.spouse1` object holds the client's full name, DOB, age, and citizenship

**Template patches for individual clients (applied April 2026).** Both `pmps-agenda.js` and `pmps-proposal.js` were updated to handle individual clients safely. The following behaviors are now conditional:

*pmps-proposal.js:*
- Estate overview paragraph: for couples with credit shelter shows combined-estate/credit-shelter language; for individuals shows single-client language with no spouse references.
- MA estate tax paragraph: for couples shows portability/first-to-die language; for individuals shows threshold-monitoring language only.
- Recommended Plan intro: drops "for each spouse" for individual clients.
- Trust description: drops "credit shelter, marital" provisions for individual clients.
- All document labels ("Pour-Over Will", "DPOA", "HCP", etc.): labeled "for each spouse" only for couples.
- Pour-Over Will description: drops "surviving spouse" reference for individual clients.
- Beneficiary designation fallback: drops "surviving spouse as primary beneficiary" for individual clients.
- Fiduciary Appointments table: renders a 2-column table (Role | Client's Appointments) for individual clients.
- Young Adult Estate Plans section: only renders if the client has adult children or `considerations.youngAdultPlans` is set.

*pmps-agenda.js:*
- Welcome text: "Welcome Denise" (not "Welcome Denise and [spouse]") for individual clients.
- Family details bullet: shows "single client; no children" vs. "married since X; N children" based on data.
- Estate tax paragraph: individual-appropriate language (no "first-to-die", no "portability between spouses").
- Tax talking points: credit shelter bullets only render for couples with `hasCreditShelter`; for individuals shows a single below-threshold note.
- Goals bullet: drops "both spouses' $2M exemptions" for individuals; uses "you become incapacitated" instead of "either spouse".
- Document list bullets: "for each spouse" label only for couples; guardian/minor-children bullet only if `hasMinorChildren`.
- Fiduciary table: 2-column layout for individuals.

If you ever encounter template errors referencing `spouse2.firstName` or `fiduciaries.spouse2[...]`, apply the same conditional guards before running.

**No legal plan:** When `hasLegalPlan` is false, the one-page summary shows flat-fee pricing instead of MetLife coverage. Scott may need to provide the fee amounts — ask if they're not in the input data.

**Estate under $2M:** Credit shelter planning isn't needed. Set `hasCreditShelter: false`. The tax scenarios will show $0 tax. The templates still work — they just won't emphasize tax savings.

**Non-resident fiduciaries:** If any named fiduciary lives outside the US (or outside Massachusetts), flag this in `fiduciaries.notes`. The agenda template has a section for discussing practical implications.

**Young adult children (18+, especially college-aged):** Once a child turns 18, parents lose automatic legal authority over medical decisions, financial accounts, and educational records (FERPA). This is a significant planning gap that most families don't realize exists. For any child 18 or older — and especially college students living away from home — recommend a young adult estate plan add-on consisting of:

- **Health Care Proxy** — Lets a parent make medical decisions if the child is incapacitated
- **Durable Power of Attorney** — Lets a parent handle financial matters (bank accounts, leases, insurance)
- **Education Power of Attorney** — Lets a parent access academic records and communicate with the school under FERPA
- **Living Will (Advance Health Directive)** — Documents the child's wishes about life-sustaining treatment
- **HIPAA Authorization** — Lets a parent access the child's medical records

Populate `considerations.youngAdultPlans` with the child's name, age, and school (if known). The agenda template will render this as a discussion item, and the proposal will include it in Additional Considerations. If pricing applies (e.g., as an optional add-on service not covered by a legal plan), include it in `legalPlan.optionalServices` or the flat-fee pricing section.
